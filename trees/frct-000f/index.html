    Locally Small Fibrations #frct-000F

    There are a number of (equivalent) variations on the definition of a locally small fibration. We attempt to provide some intuition for these definitions.

    Warmup: Locally Small Family Fibrations #frct-000G

    An ordinary category $E$ is called locally small when for any $x,y\in E$ the collection of morphisms $x\to y$ is a set. This property of $E$ can be rephrased in terms of its family fibration $\FAM{E}$ over $\SET$ as follows.

    Consider an index set $I\in \SET$ and two families $u,v\in C^I$. We may define an $I$-indexed collection $[u,v]\Sub{i\in I}$ consisting (pointwise) of all the morphisms $u\Sub{i}\to v\Sub{i}$ in $C$: $$[u,v]\Sub{i} = \brc{ f \mid f: u\Sub{i}\to v\Sub{i} }$$

    If $C$ is locally small, $[u,v]\Sub{i\in I}$ is in fact a family of sets for any $I\in\SET$ as each $[u,v]\Sub{i}$ is a set. Conversely, if $[u,v]\Sub{i\in I}$ is a family of sets for any $I\in \SET$, then $C$ is locally small as we may consider in particular the case that $I=\mathbf{1}$.

    A More Abstract Formulation of Locally Small Family Fibrations #frct-000H

    We will reformulate the local smallness property of the family fibration in a way that uses only the language that makes sense for an arbitrary cartesian fibration, though for now we stick with $\FAM{C}$. Given $u,v\in \FAM{C}[I]$, we have a “relative hom family” $[u,v]\in\Sl{\SET}{I}$, defined in warmup: locally small family fibrations. The fact that each $[u,v]\Sub{i}$ is the set of all morphisms $u\Sub{i}\to v\Sub{i}$ can be rephrased more abstractly.

    There is a canonical map $\epsilon\Sub{[u,v]}:\InvImg{[u,v]}u\to\Sub{p\Sub{[u,v]}} v$ that “evaluates” each indexing morphism $u\Sub{i}\to v\Sub{i}$.

    The Definition of Local Smallness #frct-000I

    Based on our explorations above, we are now prepared to write down (and understand) the proper definition of local smallness for an arbitrary cartesian fibration $E$ over $B$, which should be thought of as a (potentially large) category relative to $B$.

    Definition (locally small fibration) #frct-001B

    A cartesian fibration $E$ over $B$ is locally small if and only if for each $x\in B$ and $u,v\in E\Sub{x}$, the total category $\TotCat{\CandHom{x}{u}{v}}$ of hom candidates has a terminal object.
